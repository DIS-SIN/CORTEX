### CORTEX Napkin Desc

Where are we going from here.

Problem:
- Time between doing a thing and knowing what happened is too long
- When the information about the thing is known, it takes too long to know anything about it.
- When something is known about the thing, it takes too much effort to know different things about the thing.
- Understanding "the big picture" is almost impossible. How can we enable evidence-based data-driven decisions without it?
- But the real problem is data. There's a ton of it. Some of it is lost in silos. Some of it is unrelatable. It's an old story.
- Handling it becomes a major challenge. What happens when it's 3 million records. 10 million relationships.
- What happens if you want information out of it in seconds?
- If our data is stored in inflexible ways, we will never be able to get to the big picture.
- We dont want to be "just another silo"

Improve one small part:
- Keeping with survey information, we wanted to see what we could see.
- All our information is in tables right now. Like ledger books by candlelight.
- But our information isnt that. It's a network of relationships and sensations.
- Learners and knowledge. Seeing where people are gravitating to. What's influencing people.
- We wanted: an evolutionary step forward

Hypothesis:
- What if we could leave everything where it was and still manage to get it to be a flexible and powerful tool
- What if we could stream data in and out of this data superhighway
- What if we just built the darned thing and showed it to you.
- Using a graph database and high performance data pipeline technology we could accomplish three things:
1. Enable the Evalhalla system to support real load (all of the schools users).
2. Enable true big data analytics potential (including three-dimensional explorable data forests like a galaxy of stars)
3. Create a platform on which a suite of applications could connect
- We wanted the next-gen of CSPS data flow. Something that could stream information to visualizers.
- We wanted to be able to get incredible value for 30 lines of code.

Evolutionary:
- 3 million nodes 10 million records. Less than 2 seconds to get a visualization of the information
- We dont define the data structures. We extract them from the data itself. (Like filleting a fish instead of assembling one)
- We can support an ecosystem of dashboards or applications (consumers and producers of the data pipeline)

CSPS+GoC Direct Benefit
- No siloes. Built to be transferrable. departments can set up their own constellations. But they arent siloes. They can be streamed together if the analysis needs it.
- Infrastructure to collect, process, distribute to those who need the data, then embedding intelligence into that.
- Being able to see how the daily interactions and modifications to the data change things in the moment.

- Reduces required effort to create or modify complex forms to seconds.
